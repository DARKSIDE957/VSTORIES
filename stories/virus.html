Under The Noise

by Virus
I am Virus. You can call me GADDAFI if you want. I used to think names could hold a person steady, like a handle on a heavy box. Some days that works. Other days the box has no handles and the weight is all on the fingers. For a long time I helped people. I gave time. I gave energy I did not have. I answered calls that came too late at night and too early in the morning. I stood next to friends in rooms where words were not enough. I became good at hearing the small breaks in a voice when someone is trying to be strong. I thought that if I kept showing up, life would make a shape that made sense. Now I am the one who needs help. That sentence is hard to write and harder to say out loud. It feels like failing a promise I made in a quiet room to no one in particular. I have a lot of friends. If you counted names on my phone it would look like I am surrounded. But I can walk through a crowd and feel like a ghost walking through warm air. The weight I carried for others learned my shape and now it rides my shoulders even when I try to put it down. I feel negativity like weather. It is in the way eyes slide away in a store, in the tight little smiles that say good luck without the good. It sticks to me after I leave a room. I take it home and it sits on the table like a second plate. You would not see it if you came over. You would feel it anyway. There are days when the mind gets loud for no real reason. I laugh at the wrong times because the pressure needs an exit and laughter is faster than crying. I joke that I am a psycho because it is easier to make a performance of it than to name what is actually wrong. The truth is simpler. I am tired. I am scared of what loneliness can teach a person if you let it be the only teacher. I am scared of crowds and I am scared of empty rooms. Both feel like mirrors that show different kinds of distance. I know what I like and what I fear. I like honesty even when it stings. I like blood in a story because it means something alive is at stake. That does not make me cruel. It reminds me that life is not a clean line. It is a pulse. It is messy. It is proof that we are still here. The pressure in my mind comes from trying to make messy things behave like clean math. People ask how I am and I say fine because that is what the world can hold without spilling. The better answer is this. I am surviving. I am learning to ask for help without making it a debt. I am trying to be gentle with a self that I do not always understand. I am trying not to disappear behind the role of helper. I am learning that being strong is not the same as being silent. Some mornings I test the world by walking outside early. No crowds. No noise. The sky is a low gray and the light is kind to harsh edges. I walk slow. I name the things I see in my head like I am teaching a child language again. Tree. Light pole. Window. Bird. Step. Breath. When my chest tightens, I count the breaths anyway. In through the nose. Out through the mouth. I do not rush the count. I let the numbers be soft. I think about the friends I helped. I think about the ones who stayed and the ones who did not. I try not to judge either group. People have their own heavy boxes to carry. Sometimes they do not have hands free. Sometimes they do not have hands left. I wish them well from a distance and it feels like putting a small candle in a large dark hall. The candle does not change the whole room. It changes the circle around it. My circle is small most days. It is still a circle. I look for small proofs that I am not alone. A message that says I thought of you, no question attached. A voice note with static and a real laugh at the end. A coffee that tastes the way it should. A night where I fall asleep before my mind can start the loop of what if and why. None of those are miracles. All of them are enough to make the next hour lighter. This is not a sad story only. It is not a victory speech either. It is a record. It is me saying I am here. I am writing this from a room that is quiet in a way that does not scare me tonight. I have a window open a little and the air is cool. A car passes and I do not flinch. I have been worse than this. I have been better than this. I am both, and I am still moving. If you know me as the person who helps, keep that. It is true. Add this to it. I am a person who needs help sometimes. I am a person who has to sit down and breathe and admit that I cannot carry every weight, not because I am weak but because that is how bodies work. If you want to help, do not give me a speech. Give me a quiet room and twenty minutes without a story about how someone else had it worse. Sit with me. Let the silence be a bridge instead of a wall. I do not know if life has always been this way or if this is a new thing in the shape of the world. People tell me it has always been hard. People tell me it has never been this hard. Both can be true in different mouths. What I know is that pressure builds when too many feelings have no exit. So here is one exit. Words on a page. A door I can open and walk through without leaving the room. If you read this and see yourself in it, I hope you take one thing with you. You are not the only person who knows how to help and forgets how to be helped. You are not a machine that breaks because you needed care. You are a person. You can like sharp things in stories and still be gentle in real life. You can be a name people call when they are falling and you can still ask for a hand when the floor tilts for you. I am Virus. GADDAFI if you want. I am still here. I am still learning how to be a person in a body that holds more feeling than I asked for. I am practicing the small skills that keep a day steady. Drink water. Answer a friend. Ignore the phone when it is late and the voice in my head tells me I owe the world more than I have. Step outside. Name the things I see. Count, but do not turn the count into a cage. Maybe one day I will walk through a crowded place and feel ordinary again. Maybe I will not. Either way, I am building a life that makes room for the noise and the quiet both. I am building a life where help moves in both directions. If you see me out there, nod once. I will know what it means. I will nod back. Then we will both keep going, lighter by one small proof that we are seen.
